Marc McGovern

I have been thoroughly impressed with Marc as I have seen him talk at Cambridge candidate events. The key thing I want to describe about him is his passionate reasonableness. When a candidate takes a position that's stupid, Marc will calmly but emphatically explain why it's stupid, who would be hurt by it, and the more reasonable thing he would do instead.

Marc is a supporter of raising the Cambridge minimum wage to $15 per hour, something that we need given the high cost of living here.

He advocates growth and density, but he's sure to clarify that some development is harmful -- particularly speculation that won't increase the number of livable places. He'll use zoning and ordinances not as a weapon against new development, but a way for the City to encourage the kind of development it wants, even when it's not the most profitable thing.

Drag up if McGovern's passionate reasonableness makes you wish you could give someone a vote higher than #1.

Drag down if you don't support the minimum wage increase.

Leland Cheung

If you are a renter in Cambridge, or if you have friends who rent and you don't want them to have to move out of Cambridge the next time they move, you should support Leland Cheung, because he is the strongest advocate of your interests.

Cheung is a champion of new residential construction, to a greater extent than the rest of his slate. Whatever is on the table, Cheung will be pushing for zoning that allows additional height, additional density, and additional units set aside as affordable as part of the deal, because the housing crunch is a vast problem and we can't solve it by being timid. He'll compromise, but he'll be sure to establish what we want first.

I have heard people shouting at Cheung at campaign events, "Why are you building luxury housing?", as if he's the one making it expensive. New housing in Cambridge is so valuable and so expensive because there isn't enough of it. Its price is a sign that we're filling demand and that there's a long way to go. We need higher density in appropriate places, before all housing turns into luxury housing and the soul of the city disappears.

Not all of Cheung's platform is about building. In his time on the council, he's pushed for the expansion of Zipcar and the introduction of Hubway into the city. He also aims to make the government more accessible through information technology, although so far, useful improvements in the city's IT have been slow to appear.

Drag up if you care about the city's information technology and you think Cheung will finally make it work.

Drag down if towers in Kendall Square make you uncomfortable.
